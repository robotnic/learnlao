# Lesson 1.1.1 | Greetings & Politeness

**Path**: Path 1 - Foundations (Transliteration & Survival)  
**Level**: 1.1 - Greetings & Survival Essentials  
**Duration**: 45 minutes  
**Proficiency Goal**: Recognize and produce basic greetings and politeness formulas (CEFR A1.1)  

### Intro Text

Welcome to Lao! In this first lesson, you'll learn how to greet people and show respect through polite language. These essential phrases will help you make a great first impression and start conversations naturally. You'll practice saying "sabaidee" (hello), thanking people, and showing gratitude the Lao way.

**Learning Objectives**:
- [ ] Recognize and understand 10 basic greetings and politeness phrases
- [ ] Produce basic greetings with correct pronunciation and tone
- [ ] Understand cultural context of Lao politeness (wai gesture, respectful speech)
- [ ] Apply greetings appropriately in different contexts (formal vs. casual)

## Learning Activities

### Game Selection

**Activity Flow**:
1. Intro + Flashcard review (5 min)
2. Listening Discrimination game (5 min)
3. Speech Recognition game (10 min)
4. Quiz (10 min)
5. Celebration (5 min)

---

## Learning Structure

### Session 1: Introduction & Recognition (Day 1)
- **Duration**: 15 minutes
- **Content**: 
  - Show all 10 phrases with audio + Lao context
  - Play short video showing "wai" gesture and greeting scenarios
  - Introduce romanization system
- **Activity**: Flashcards - tap greeting to hear audio + see English translation
- **Accuracy Target**: 70%+ (no penalties, learning phase)

### Session 2: Listening Discrimination (Day 2-3)
- **Duration**: 15 minutes
- **Content**: Review phrases (60% new, 40% from Session 1)
- **Activity**: Listen to audio, select correct greeting from 3 options
- **Accuracy Target**: 75%+ (feedback on errors)

### Session 3: Production & Consolidation (Day 3-4)
- **Duration**: 15 minutes
- **Content**: Heavy review (80% previous, 20% new details)
- **Activity**: Speech Recognition - say the greeting aloud, app judges pronunciation
- **Accuracy Target**: 80%+ (clear success/failure feedback)

---

## Assessment Quiz

**Format**: 5 multiple choice + 3 speaking items  
**Passing Score**: 80%+  
**Time**: 10 minutes

### Sample Quiz Questions

1. **Recognition**: You hear "khop jai" - what does it mean?
   - A) Goodbye
   - B) Thank you
   - C) Hello
   - D) Goodbye formal
   - *Answer: B*

2. **Recognition**: What is the most common Lao greeting?
  - A) La kon
  - B) Khop jai
  - C) Sabaidee
  - D) Bo sabaai
   - *Answer: C*

3. **Context**: A person greets you with "sabaidee" - what should you respond?
  - A) Bo ao, khop jai
  - B) Men, khop jai
   - C) Sabaidee (or ask about their day)
  - D) Bo
   - *Answer: C*

4. **Production (Speaking)**: Say "hello" in Lao
   - *Expected: Sabaidee (with similar tones)*

5. **Production (Speaking)**: Say "thank you very much"
  - *Expected: Khop jai lai*

6. **Application**: Someone thanks you. You want to refuse politely. What do you say?
  - A) Bo
  - B) Bo ao, khop jai
  - C) Men, khop jai
   - D) Sabaidee
   - *Answer: B*

7. **Production (Speaking)**: Say goodbye
  - *Expected: La kon*

8. **Listening**: Identify which phrase means "No, thank you"
  - Plays audio of: "bo ao, khop jai"
  - *Expected: Bo ao, khop jai*

## Pedagogical Notes

**Why these games?**
- **Flashcards**: Simple, low-pressure introduction to sounds and meanings. Audio reinforces pronunciation patterns.
- **Listening Discrimination**: Develops ability to distinguish similar phonemes (important for tonal languages). Builds confidence in listening skills.
- **Speech Recognition**: Bridges from passive recognition to active production. Immediate feedback on pronunciation accuracy builds confidence early.

**Difficulty progression**:
- Session 1: Visual + Audio (multiple cues)
- Session 2: Audio only + Multiple choice (reduced cues, timed pressure)
- Session 3: Speak freely (no cues, evaluates pronunciation)

**Motivation factors**:
- Immediate relevance: First greetings are immediately useful
- Quick wins: Simple 10-phrase set builds confidence
- Cultural connection: Context about "wai" gesture makes learning meaningful
- Dice progress (âš€ â†’ âš â†’ âš‚) visible after each session
- Voice feedback in celebration adds personal touch

**Tone language consideration**:
- Lao has 6 tones; this lesson uses simple greetings with consistent tones
- Speech Recognition game will flag tone errors for learner awareness
- No explicit tone teaching in Lesson 1 (save for Level 2.2)
